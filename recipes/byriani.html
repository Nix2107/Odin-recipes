Byriani Recipe

Description:

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. It is made with Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, prawn, fish)
or in some cases without any meat, and sometimes, in addition, eggs and potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 300g basmati rice
  • 25g butter
  • one large onion, finely sliced
  • one bay leaf
  • three cardamom pods
  • small cinnamon stick
  • one teaspoon of turmeric
  • four skinless chicken breasts, cut into large chunks
  • four tablespoons of balti curry paste
  • 85g raisins
  • 850ml chicken stock
  • 30g coriander, ½ chopped, ½ leaves picked and 2 tbsp toasted flaked almonds

Steps:

  1. Soak 300g basmati rice in warm water, then wash in cold until the water runs clear.
  2. Heat 25g butter in a saucepan and cook 1 finely sliced large onion with 1 bay leaf, 3 cardamom pods and 1 small cinnamon for 10 mins.
  3. Sprinkle in one teaspoon of turmeric, then add four chicken breasts
  4. Add four tablespoons of curry paste, and cook until aromatic.
  5. Stir the rice into the pan with 85g raisins, then pour over 850ml chicken stock.
  6. Place a tight-fitting lid on the pan and bring to a hard boil, then lower the heat to a minimum and cook the rice for another 5 mins.
  7. Turn off the heat and leave for 10 mins. Stir well, mixing through 15g chopped coriander.
